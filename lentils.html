flexible curried lentils

ingredients

absolute essentials

  • lentils (red are my fav, green also works)
  • onion
  • garlic
  • curry spices -- a mix can be good
  • other spices: salt, pepper, paprika, cinnamon, cumin, chilli powder, bay leaf

nice to haves

  • any veg you want (peas + potatoes are v nice)
  • any meat if you want
  • chicken or veg broth
  • tomatoes -- diced, canned, whatever
  • ginger
  • yogurt
  • coconut oil

recipe

** note! this is a super flexible and fun recipe. taste as you go. you'll figure out what ~ feels right in your heart ~

** if you want meat -- esp chicken -- a fun marinade is yogurt with spices, salt, pepper, and garlic. depending on the size of your meat chunks, throw it in before or after the veg. i usually like to do it after so it doesn't get overcooked.

** it's a flexible recipe. what you basically need is a spice mix you like and a fat source. greek yogurt rounds out the flavor really nicely but you can experiment with coconut oil and such.

  1. start to cook the onions! you can cook them in butter, canola oil, or coconut oil.
  2. season the onions!! with the spices everything is really what you want it to be. (if you have a curry mix that's what you should put the most in.) a nice trick is to put a good amount of spice in with the onions -- beacuse it's going to go a longer way and tastes better if you add it at this stage rather than later. make sure the heat isn't too high.
  3. once your onions are starting to cook, throw in some garlic!
  4. if you have uncooked veg, now is a great time to start to add it. spice and taste as you go.
  5. after the onions, garlic, and possible veg have cooked, start adding the tomatoes. idk how much you should put in. put what feels right. remember to keep tasting and spicing.
  6. add the yogurt if you have it. milk also works.
  7. IS SHIT COOKING AND SOLID? fab. put in those mother fucking lentils. add water or broth. let it simmer. cover or don't.
  8. tbh i have no idea how long lentils actually cook for. feel like it's 20-30 minutes usually. you can keep taste testing until the lentils feel soft.
